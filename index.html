The Problem — why pickles haven't moved with modern life

Traditional pickles are beloved — but they’re not built for the mobile, health-conscious generation. Fragile glass jars, heavy oil, artificial preservatives and no portion control make them inconvenient for students, travellers and modern professionals.

Key pain points

  • Breakable glass packaging — not travel-friendly.
  • High oil content, palm oil & preservatives like sodium benzoate.
  • Large jars — no portion control for single users or travellers.
  • Conventional pickles are rarely used as a spread, marinade or cooking base.

Who feels this pain?

Primary consumers:

  • Students, hostellers & travellers (18–30)
  • Young working professionals in Tier-1 & Tier-2 cities
  • B2B: HORECA — cloud kitchens, cafes, corporate kitchens

Our answer — Svachaar

Svachaar modernises the pickle: small-batch, chemical-free, protein-infused pickles in leak-proof biodegradable zipper pouches — designed to travel, to spread, and to be used as a modern cooking ingredient.

Core promises

  • Protein-infused variants for the health conscious.

FAQ — quick answers

Are these pickles preservative-free?

Yes — no sodium benzoate or artificial preservatives. We rely on small-batch freshness and measured refrigeration / packaging to ensure safety.

How long do travel packs stay fresh?

Travel packs (50g) are shelf-stable for a shorter window than commercial jars; recommended to consume within 30–60 days after opening for best flavour. We provide clear expiry & storage guidance on each pack.

Are pouches really biodegradable?

We use certified biodegradable laminate pouches designed for food use — we will publish supplier certificates and disposal instructions on packaging.
